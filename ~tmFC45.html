Welcome!

I am a PhD Candidate in Linguistics and a data science instructor at UC Berkeley. My research focuses on understanding and improving speech representations. I am particularly interested in using deep learning to generate powerful, complex, and interpretable representations of speech.

I have been teaching data science and developing curriculum since 2019 as a part of the UC Berkeley D-Lab. I specialize in participant engagement, revising curriculum, and instructor mentoring.

Current Projects

Neural networks for phonetics

My work focuses on understanding neural net representations of speech, in particular their ability to encode fine-grained acoustic information. In addition, I propose a new approach to neural net representations in phonetics that balances performance and interpretability.

Acoustic representations

My dissertation focuses on defining the key properties of effective phonetic representations. This includes both quantitative performance and qualitative factors that influence adoption and choice of acoutic parameters.

Data science and pedagogy

Data science methods have become more and more central to social science research, but training in these methods is not yet common practice. I am passionate about helping scientists expand their access to data science and computational methods through formal and informal instruction.

Unsupervised vowel clustering

Machine learning methods show promise for supplementing traditional analyses. This project explores alternatives to the ubiquitous K-means for vowel clustering. Collaboration with Jennifer Kuo(UCLA)
